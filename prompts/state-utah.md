# Utah State Tax Reference (Tax Year 2025)

## Rate
Flat 4.55% on all Utah taxable income.

## Starting Point
Utah taxable income = federal AGI (line 11 of 1040), with Utah-specific additions and subtractions applied.

## Key Additions to Federal AGI
- None significant for most filers (Utah conforms closely to federal)

## Key Subtractions from Federal AGI
- **State tax refund**: If included in federal income, subtract
- **Retirement income credit**: See taxpayer credit below
- **Railroad retirement**: Exempt
- **Social Security**: Exempt to the extent included in federal AGI (Utah provides a nonrefundable credit — see below)

## Utah Taxpayer Credit
Utah applies a nonrefundable personal exemption credit that effectively creates a 0% bracket at the bottom:
- **Credit amount**: 6% of the federal standard deduction or itemized deductions (whichever the taxpayer claims on the federal return), plus 6% of personal exemptions equivalent ($876 per exemption for 2025)
- **Phase-out**: Credit reduced by 1.3 cents per dollar of AGI exceeding $16,365 (single) / $32,731 (MFJ)
- **Effect**: For high-income filers (AGI >$100K+), the credit is fully phased out and the effective rate approaches the full 4.55%

## Utah Itemized vs Standard
Utah does NOT have its own standard deduction. The taxpayer credit references the federal deduction amount. Utah taxpayers do not separately choose standard vs itemized for state purposes.

## SALT Interaction
- Utah income tax paid is deductible on the federal Schedule A as part of SALT (subject to the $40K/$10K cap)
- This creates a circular calculation: higher Utah tax → higher federal SALT deduction → lower federal tax, but SALT cap limits the benefit for most filers in this product's target market
- For the loop: when estimating total tax (federal + state), note that loopholes reducing AGI reduce both federal AND Utah tax (4.55% state savings on top of federal marginal rate savings)

## Utah-Specific Loopholes
| ID | Loophole | Detail |
|----|----------|--------|
| UT_529 | Utah 529 Credit | 4.55% credit on contributions to Utah 529 (my529), max $2,290 single / $4,580 MFJ credit (contributions up to ~$50K single / ~$100K MFJ). Nonrefundable. |
| UT_HOME_ENERGY | Utah Home Energy Credit | Limited credits for residential energy; check current availability |
| UT_PTET | Utah PTET Election | Pass-through entities can elect to pay Utah tax at entity level (4.55%), owner gets offsetting credit. Bypasses federal SALT cap for state tax on business income. |
| UT_RETIRE_CREDIT | Utah Retirement Credit | Credit for SS benefits and certain retirement income; phased out at higher AGI |

## Utah Conflict Rules
- UT_PTET only available for pass-through entities (S-Corp, partnership, LLC taxed as partnership)
- UT_529 credit is nonrefundable — cannot reduce Utah tax below zero
- UT_PTET interacts with federal DED_SALT — the PTET payment is treated as a business deduction federally, not a SALT itemized deduction, effectively bypassing the SALT cap

## Estimation
- For most filers in target market (AGI >$100K): Utah tax ≈ AGI × 4.55% (taxpayer credit is fully or nearly phased out)
- Any loophole that reduces AGI saves an additional 4.55% at the state level
- UT_PTET can unlock significant savings for pass-through owners hitting the federal SALT cap
- UT_529 is a straightforward credit worth up to $4,580/year for MFJ — low-hanging fruit if not already utilized
